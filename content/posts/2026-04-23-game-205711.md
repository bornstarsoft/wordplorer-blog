The global extended reality (XR) market is undergoing a significant transformation, primarily driven by the rapid adoption of smart glasses. In 2025, XR device shipments surged by an impressive 44.4% year-over-year, with lightweight, AI-enabled eyewear leading the charge. This shift indicates a growing consumer preference for more versatile and user-friendly devices, moving away from traditional bulky virtual and mixed reality headsets.

Meta continues to dominate the market with a substantial 72.2% share, thanks to its innovative partnerships and product launches. However, the decline in shipments of its Quest headset lineup highlights the challenges faced by VR and MR headsets, as demand narrows to gaming-centric applications. As we look ahead, the XR landscape is poised for further evolution, with expectations that display-enabled smart glasses will become the norm by 2027.
