Fifteen years ago, Capcom released a game that would redefine the puzzle genre: Ghost Trick. Created by Shu Takumi, the mind behind the beloved Ace Attorney series, this inventive title offers players a unique blend of narrative and gameplay that remains unmatched in the industry. As you navigate the afterlife as Sissel, a spirit seeking to uncover the mystery of his own death, you engage with a vibrant cast of characters and clever puzzles that challenge your wit and creativity.

What truly sets Ghost Trick apart is its seamless integration of story and mechanics. Each puzzle you solve not only advances the plot but also deepens your connection to the characters, from the charming detective Lynne to the flamboyant Cabanela. The games ability to weave humor and tragedy into its narrative creates a rich experience that resonates with players long after the credits roll.

As we celebrate 15 years of Ghost Trick, its clear that this title has left an indelible mark on gaming. Its experimental nature and heartfelt storytelling invite us to reflect on the importance of life and the connections we forge.
